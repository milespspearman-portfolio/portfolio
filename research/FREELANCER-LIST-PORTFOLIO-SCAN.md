# PATTERNS — what these peers actually do (tallies from the 50 read, 46 fully)

**1. Page order.** The dominant template, by a wide margin (~30 of 46 fully-read sites), is:
`[name/one-line tagline] → work grid immediately → about (separate page, late in nav) → contact (nav-last item and/or footer email)`.
Nav order is remarkably uniform: **Work first (or homepage IS the work), About second-to-last, Contact last.** Nobody puts About before Work. Several (Lawless, Camila, Emma Simon, Laura Mechling, Arcy, Even Odd) have essentially NO homepage text beyond a single tagline line.

**2. Metrics: 1 shows, 49 hide.** Exactly **one** person in the entire sheet — Benjamin Apple (writer/producer) — displays performance numbers ("2M organic views," "64M organic views on Meta," "10X average engagement"). **Zero** of the 17 editors/DPs/videographers, zero of the 10 production companies, zero of the motion designers, zero of the CDs/ADs show a view count, play count, or engagement stat anywhere. Credibility is instead carried by: client/brand names in project titles (~35 of 47 assessable sites), client logo walls (East Peak, Genevieve Rico, Puzzle World, Dave Haller), festival laurels (Puzzle World), or press coverage (Benjamin Apple).

**3. Lead element: grid ≫ text ≫ reel.** Of 46 fully-read sites: **~30 lead with a work grid/list**, **~12 lead with a text/bio block** (Matty Rich's enter-gate, Whiskers Akimbo, Min, East Peak, Christie Goshe, Emani Sunday, Benjamin Apple, papercats, Genevieve Rico's logo wall...), and only **3 lead with a playable reel** (Matthew Psyllos, Alisia Perez, Ryan Grandmaison).
