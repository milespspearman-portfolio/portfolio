## 1. Copy patterns from the reference (audrey-kelly.com)

Studied: `/sbux-campaign` (Starbucks Fall campaign) and `/adobe-express` (Adobe Express social case study) — the two reachable project pages linked from her homepage/marketing-portfolio.

**Structure — Challenge → Output, not Challenge/Insight/Idea/Results.** Neither page uses a classic four-part case-study arc. `/adobe-express` states it almost literally: *"Challenge: Audit Adobe Express's social media content..."* → *"Output: new, fresh look and feel..."* `/sbux-campaign` compresses even further — one sentence of creative intent, no separate insight/idea beat: *"For Starbucks Fall campaign in 2022, we created content that resonated with fall fanatics and PSL-skeptics alike."* Context is given in a single breath, not unpacked section by section.

**Role is claimed in first person, singular, at the top — then the "we" appears once the work itself is described.** Both pages open with an unambiguous "My role" / "In my role as..." line naming a title and a scope of responsibility (*"My role: Associate Creative Director, Starbucks: creative direction and strategic feedback from concept to final product, photo and art direction (pre pro and on set), feedback and approvals"*; *"In my role as Creative Strategist and Producer at Adobe, I oversaw the development of all owned social content for the product"*). The shift to "we created" happens only when narrating the finished work, not when claiming credit for it.

**Sentences are short and declarative, capped around ~20-25 words, and metrics (when used) are specific, not vague.** `/adobe-express` states *"37.8% increase in organic following on Instagram"* and *"1100% on YouTube"* — exact figures, stated plainly, with no hedging language. `/sbux-campaign` uses zero metrics at all — it's presented as conceptual/creative work only. The throughline: state a number or don't, but never round-and-qualify ("significant increase," "strong engagement").

**Conspicuously absent from both:** budget, timeline, client quotes, competitor mentions, and (on `/sbux-campaign`) any quantified result whatsoever. Team members are credited as executors of the described work, not as co-authors — the "I" holds the directorial claim even when "we" made it.
