# Copy Principles — the gate every site copy change runs through

Sources: audrey-kelly.com case studies (verified directly Jul 4 2026), 11-site field scan (`research/FIELD-SCAN-CD-PRODUCER-PORTFOLIOS.md`), Miles's resume-tailoring practice (honest-qualification rule, quantified real experience), site data rules (`CLAUDE.md`), and — folded Jul 4 — Miles's resume + LinkedIn sweeps, extracted verbatim with per-quote sources in `research/COPY-INPUTS-RESUME-LINKEDIN.md` (shipped resume PDF, resume-coverletter-sweep, linkedin-strategy-chatsweep). See the "Resume & LinkedIn alignment" section below for the rules derived from it.

## The 7 checks (run ALL before any copy ships)

1. **True and on-record.** Every noun, number, name is verifiable from the `portfolio` array, Miles's resume, or Miles's own words. On-screen stats are DERIVED in code, never typed. Role claims are résumé-grade — Miles confirms each once, then they're locked.
2. **Claim first-person, describe as "we."** The Audrey pattern: role line opens first-person and specific ("Concepted, scripted, hosted & creatively directed by Miles Spearman"), the work description may use "we" for team contexts. Credit is claimed before it's shared — never the reverse.
3. **Exact or absent.** Numbers appear precise (728.4K, 2.6M) or not at all. Banned: "significant," "strong," "many," "viral" (unless quoting a number), any hedge or inflation. A missing metric beats a vague one.
4. **Two beats, short sentences.** Challenge → Output. No four-part case-study arcs. Target ≤22 words per sentence; cut role notes out of titles (they live in `role` fields).
5. **Proof adjacency.** Every claim sits next to its playable evidence — this site's structural advantage. If a sentence can't sit near its video, question the sentence.
6. **Miles's words win.** Claude drafts are labeled drafts; anything unverifiable carries `[MILES: confirm]`; his edits are never overwritten, only appended-to with labeled alternates. (Standing rule from his memory system.)
7. **Ship-check.** Final read against: locked-copy list untouched? meta-text leaked? names/tunes/brands spelled right (O'Hara-class errors)? Then commit.

## Register reference (calibrated from sources)
- Role lines: title + concrete responsibilities, plainly strung ("creative direction and strategic feedback from concept to final product") — length OK here, precision required.
- Descriptions: confident, cultural, concrete ("leaned into culture and fandom, celebrated the return of cult favorites") — no adjectives doing a number's job.
- Field norm is metric-silence; Miles INVERTS this deliberately (play counts everywhere) because he's selling growth outcomes for staff roles, not freelance vibe. Keep the inversion; apply check #3 to it.

## Resume & LinkedIn alignment (folded Jul 4)

All quotes and sources verbatim in `research/COPY-INPUTS-RESUME-LINKEDIN.md`. These rules are additive to the 7 checks; where they collide, the stricter reading wins.

1. **Adobe title = the shipped PDF's title, exactly.** If the site states his Adobe title, it is "Social Lead, Creator & Community | Adobe Brand" (ladder: Social Coordinator Jul 2023–Jan 2024 → Social Producer Jan 2024–May 2025 → Social Lead May 2025–Present). Never bare "Social Lead" ("fuck no! never put social lead in any of my resumes" — resume sweep chat 07); descriptive lowercase phrases ("social producer and content creator at Adobe Brand") are fine, contradicting title claims are not. His real internal title is "Social Lead, Real-Time & Community" — don't surface it unless he asks. (Sources: `public/Miles-Spearman-Resume.pdf`; COPY-INPUTS §1a–1b.)
2. **The 225k number always carries its resume context and never reads as Adobe work.** On-record form: "Grew two TikTok accounts from 0 to 225k+ followers in 4 months" at Branded (@OTOTO / @KeyNutrients). Miles: "this needs to be distinguished that its not from Adobe." (Sources: PDF; COPY-INPUTS §2.)
3. **Site numbers must come from the shipped PDF or the `portfolio` array.** PDF-cleared set: 4+ years · 0→225k+/4 months · 8 priority channels · three @UofCincy series · GPA 3.94/4.0. Variant-only numbers (250+ videos, 50+ monthly, 1M+ employees, 7,000+ viewers/22%, 10+ influencers, $25k scholarship) need Miles's OK first. "40% QoQ" is dead — he removed it everywhere ("i NEVER I HATE WHEN YOU JUST THROW THE QOQ 40%"); and no stat-badge grids ("hate this entire thing"). (Sources: COPY-INPUTS §2, §4.2–4.3.)
4. **Never pair quick-turn/same-day language with executive content.** "exec things deserve to have time to go through the write approvals" — quick-turn belongs to event coverage and activations only; exec work = prep, discretion, approvals. (Source: COPY-INPUTS §4.4, resume sweep chat 14.)
5. **No em dashes in any copy Miles signs.** "no em dashes you know that" (LinkedIn chat 18); resume workflow codifies "No em dashes — use commas." Use commas, colons, parentheses, periods. (Source: COPY-INPUTS §4.5.)
6. **NDA line: the site says nothing about Adobe work beyond the shipped resume + the public reels.** His standing ask: "Help me do something different that won't be get me in trouble with NDA"; his pillar goal: "make your job legible + impressive without NDA issues." No internal metrics, tools, processes, or unreleased anything. (Source: COPY-INPUTS §3, §4.7.)
7. **Voice = strategic insider, confident without arrogant — insight, not formatting.** His director's bar, adopted as doctrine: "Most of this stuff isn't insight. It's formatting." No listicle-speak, no safe-take filler; claims sit on research and playable proof. When drafting copy alternates for Miles, give exactly 3 options in his voice ("i need like three hook options moving forward… you know my tone would never say that"). Never invent personal narrative around sensitive dynamics. (Source: COPY-INPUTS §3, §4.8.)
8. **Jazz stays one line inside the professional story, framed as creator fluency.** Resume form is canonical: "Off the clock I'm a creator myself… keeps me fluent in how creators actually build an audience"; his Feb rule: "Jazz/music: One line in the About bio only. Not its own section." The site's "Off the Clock" sidebar is the Miles-approved exception — don't let jazz leak into work-section copy. Bio/education lines reuse his own written sentence (BBA + BA, Media Production minor, 3.94 GPA, "rhythm, pacing, and storytelling"). (Sources: PDF; COPY-INPUTS §3.)
